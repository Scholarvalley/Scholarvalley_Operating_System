Services

Scholarvalley supports students in improving their chances of studying in partner universities and accessing scholarship opportunities. Our process follows three main steps:

1. Document Review

The Document Review allows our team to evaluate your academic profile and determine your potential eligibility for scholarships.

Fees:

  • $5 – Prospective Freshman
  • $10 – Continuing Undergraduate Student
  • $15 – Graduate Student

During this stage, Scholarvalley reviews your academic documents and profile. Based on this review, we generate a statistical percentage indicating your likelihood of receiving scholarship opportunities through our partner institutions.

2. Application Support

Once the document review is completed, students may proceed with Application Support.

Fee: $250 USD

A Scholarvalley Ambassador will guide you through:

  • Preparing and submitting university applications
  • Communicating with our partner universities
  • Ensuring required documents are correctly submitted
  • Monitoring the application process

This step ensures your application is properly prepared and submitted to maximize your chances of admission.

3. Scholarship Support

If you receive a scholarship offer or qualify for scholarship opportunities, Scholarvalley provides additional support to help secure and optimize your financial aid.

Fee: $1500 USD

Scholarvalley officers assist students with:

  • Scholarship documentation and confirmation
  • Communication with partner universities regarding funding
  • Guidance through scholarship acceptance procedures

Note: Scholarship support may also include strategies to help students qualify for in-state tuition rates instead of international student fees, when possible through partner institutions. This can significantly reduce the overall cost of study.

Students must notify their Scholarvalley Officer immediately upon receiving any scholarship offer so proper support can be provided.

Important

The document review helps determine eligibility, but it does not guarantee admission or scholarship awards, as final decisions are made by partner universities and scholarship providers.
